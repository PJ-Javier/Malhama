Fujairah Fort

Fujairah Fort is a fort in the city of Fujairah, United Arab Emirates. Dating back to the 16th century, it is among the oldest as well as the largest castles in the country. It is noted for playing significant roles in fighting back the wave of colonialism. Today, it is among the main tourist attractions in the city. It is probably the oldest fort in the UAE and was occupied by the Wahhabists. It is located about 2 kilometres from central modern Fujairah in the old Fujairah region, on a small, 20-metre rocky hill. The fort itself is a part of a complex that includes several houses and a mosque. It is guarded by four watchtowers, connected by walls. Three of the watchtowers were destroyed by British forces in 1925 during a anti-slavery policy action. These towers were restored with the same materials the rest of the fort was built with by the Fujairah Administration of Antiquity and Heritage from 1997-2000.
