SHARJAH AQUARIUM

First opening its doors in 2008, the Sharjah Aquarium features 20 tanks that recreate different local aquatic environments, from coral reefs and rock pools, to lagoons and mangroves. Enjoy a close-up look at the local species of fish and troves of knowledge on the emirate’s historic ports and coasts. The aquarium holds almost 100 species, including the clownfish, seahorse, moray eel, sea ray, and reef shark. The aquarium also holds a turtle rehabilitation program, where the husbandry team collects and receives sick and injured turtles, rehabilitates, and then releases them back to their natural habitat, the sea. As part of the Sharjah Museum Authority’s “Because We Care” initiative, the Aquarium is organizing an annual marine environment clean-up campaign to help raise awareness of environmental protection and instill the values of preserving marine attractions, and also awareness for the dangers of marine pollution that can result from irresponsible behavior.
