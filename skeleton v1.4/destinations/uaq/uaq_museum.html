UAQ National Museum

Located inside an old fort that was once the home of the emirate's ruling family, this museum is home to artifacts unearthed at the Ed Dur archaeological site, as well as showcases of the history of warfare in the region. This fort dates back to 1768, built by the founder of the Al Mualla dynasty, Rashid Bin Majid Al Mualla and continued to be the family's residence until the 1960s. Much like other early forts in the UAE, it was converted into other uses until it finally settled into Umm Al-Quwain's premier national museum in 2000. Inside the museum artifacts from Ed Dur and Tel Abraq. As it was once the residence of royalty, there are exhibits featuring memorabilia from the royal family, as well as gifts that were offered to them, including swords, coins, jewelry, and a matchlock pistol. Other exhibits include photos that showcase the emirate's history, antiquated sea equipment, documents, and military equipment such as old cannons. There is also a traditional market exhibit called the Attar. The museum is open from 8:00 AM to 2:00 PM on Saturdays to Thursdays, and 5:00 PM to 8:00 PM on Fridays. This museum is located in the old souq area of the emirate Madeena Al Qadeema.
