Falaj Al Mualla

30 kilometres from the center of Umm Al Quwain city, Falaj Al Mualla (originally Falaj Al Ali) is an old inland oasis town built in the 19th century. This town was founded by the tribe that resided in Al Sinniyah, the Al Ali, who moved where the town would be founded due to a lack of water resources. This town attracts visitors due to its historical structures, such as the fort and the three towers that protect and look over the fertile wadi. The fort was constructed in 1825 by the ruler of Umm Al Quwain at the time, Sheikh Abdulla Bin Rashid Al Mualla, and now houses its own museum, Al Hasem, and a mosque. The fort is open from 8 AM to 2 PM, reopens at 3 PM, , and closes at 8 PM from Mondays to Thursdays. It is only open from 3 PM to 8 PM on Fridays. The towers were also constructed by Sheikh Abdulla Bin Rashid Al Mualla in the 19th century and restored in 2007.
