Al Sinniyah Island

Situated 19 kilometres from Umm Al Quwain, Al Sinniyah (or Senneyah Island) is a serene group of islands near the coast of the main city of the emirate. A part of the Ajman Tour Package, it is one of the most popular places to visit in Umm Al Quwain. Surrounded by mangroves, Al Sinniyah is an important location for migratory birds. Various desert flora can be found here as well, like Ghaf trees. The bird species that can be observed here include flamingos, herns, herons, and plovers, often found in the shallow mud flats. The timings are from 10 AM to 6 PM, and entry is free. There is also some history behind the place, as this was once settled by the residents of Umm Al Quwain after they moved from Mulah Island two hundred years ago. There are two ancient towers, locally named as Burj Nahar and Burj Al Bahar, which were once used to illuminate ships. On the island's eastern shore, there are remains of buildings, made out of marine stones, theorized to be the residences of fishermen due to their proximity to the water. These remains had ancient potteries, both local and foreign.
