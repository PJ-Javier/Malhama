RAK National Museum

Another Emirate national museum located in a fort, this museum is inside Dhayah Fort. This fort has a long and varied history, having been destroyed twice on two separate occasions to conflicts, once served as a place of residence for the ruling family (until it was given up in 1920, then again for the Qawasim family until 1964), then converted into a police headquarters and finally as a museum for the emirate it resides in. Some of this history plays a part in its current status as a museum; part of the artifacts on display have been donated by the Qawasim family (along with the residents of Ras Al Khaimah). Many rooms inside the Fort are open for public visit and house many archaeological, historical and ethnographical objects on exhibit. The Department of Antiquities have also contributed to this museum, their excavations, surveys and projects have added to the information and material of the museum.
