Dhayah Fort

Dhayah Fort is the only hill fort that remains in the United Arab Emirates, dating back to the Bronze Age as a settlement and fortification for the locals. The golden, mud-bricked fortress, built in the 19th century, was the site of a battle between British troops and the local Qawasimi tribes and continues to be a historical monument to this day. The fort itself is located on a 70 metre-tall hill that is situated between date palm gardens and the Jebel Jais mountains, which forms a central hub of the lush oasis. Once, this fort would have been the residence of the ruling Sheikh. The climb to the fort is uneven and rocky, keeping those with prams, wheelchairs, etc. from accessing the site. There is also no lighting, so it is recommended that the best occasion to visit is during daytime hours. At the forts base 12 large Wadi Suq tombs from 2,000 to 1,300 BC lie, four of which have already been excavated to exhibit the funerary structures of Southeastern Arabia. The RAK Department of Antiquities and Museums has plans to turn the area into an archaeology park.
