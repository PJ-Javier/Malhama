Ras Al Khaimah

HISTORY OF RAS AL KHAIMAH

With a rich history dating back for 7,000 years, in the 13th and 14th centuries, a prosperous port arose in the area now known as Ras Al Khaimah. Renamed Julfar, it became one of the most prominent trading posts in the Gulf and was renowned for its exquisite pearls.

It is home to one of the best-preserved traditional fishing villages in the Gulf, Al Jazirah Al Hamra, and the only hilltop fort still in existence in the UAE, the Dhayah Fort—18th century.

The emirate is notable for its varied topography, from the Hajar mountains, to spanning sand dunes to stretches of beaches, as well as its adventure tourism attractions. It is home to more than 38,000 businesses from 100 countries covering over 50 industries.

Explore

Ras Al Khaimah combines fascination and relaxation with its white beaches and calm blues seas, majestic desert dunes and the Hajar Mountains. It has fascinating landmarks, beautiful wildlife conservations and sanctuaries.
