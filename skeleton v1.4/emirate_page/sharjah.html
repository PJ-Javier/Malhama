HISTORY OF SHARJAH

Historically, Sharjah was one of the wealthiest areas in the gulf region with a very low population and income sources gained from economic activities such as trade, farming, hunting, fishing and pearl farming.

The town became unstable after the Portuguese occupation that sought to control the spice trade, forts of historical renown were built under their charge. After which, the Dutch tried to dominate the area for the same reasons.

In the turn of the 17th century, the British started trading with the forefathers’ of Sharjah’s ruling family. At the end of the 18th century, the relationship between two powers deteriorated due to contentions.

Upon the formation of the United Arab Emirates. The developing years brought progress to the city and with the discovery of oil and its accessible natural wealth of resources the emirate has been able to maintain and preserve its culture.
