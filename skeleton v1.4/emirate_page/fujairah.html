HISTORY OF FUJAIRAH

The Emirate of Fujairah sits at the mouth of an important trade route, the Wadi Ham, through the mountains to the interior and the Gulf Coast.

The east coast of what is now the UAE was subject to Muscat until 1850, when it was annexed by Al Qasimi of Sharjah, till they declared independence from Sharjah. At this time, the emirate consisted of some 150 houses and 3,000 date lamps and its people lived mainly through pearling and date cultivation.

On 2nd of December 1971, the emirate joined the United Arab Emirates. It’s rich archeological finds point to a history of human settlement and trading. From forts, trade routs, to being home to the oldest mosque in use in the UAE.
