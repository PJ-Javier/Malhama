Dubai

the
living
desert

As the second-largest emirate, Dubai boasts of world-class hotels, modern architecture, entertainment and dining venues, traditional markets, shopping centres and sporting events. A center of transport and commerce and a common entry point for travelers.

Ras Al Khaimah

the
top of
the tent

As an up-and-coming tourism hub, Ras Al Khaimah has scenic locations with diverse landscapes ranging from spanning sand dunes, coastal strips, salt flats, mangroves and mountains. A true nature lover's paradise.

Ajman

the
beautiful
jewel

One of the budget destinations, the smallest emirate is home to magical beaches with fresh air, a peaceful environment and clean water, providing various marine activities that attract many sea lovers.

Fujairah

the
spring
that springs

Located completely on the eastern coast of the UAE, the emirate is distinguished with its rugged terrain of mountains, valleys, waterfalls, oases and wide sandy beaches. It is known for its several hot, cold and mineral springs.

Umm Al Quwain

the
mother
of two powers

The second-smallest emirate, known for its suspension from city life, has rich coastal mangroves on the coast of Arabian Gulf and many islands that lie to the east of the mainland.

Sharjah

the
rising
sun

The emirate bears its charm with architectural heritage and cultural wealth, known for its various touristic sites, which include historical areas like Heart of Sharjah and modern sites like Al Qasba and Al Majaz Waterfront. Sharjah is another budget destination.

Abu Dhabi

the
land of
the gazelle

The captial city has become a centre for world-class sporting events such as: the Abu Dhabi Golf Championship, the prestigious Abu Dhabi Desert Challenge cross-country rally and the Formula One Abu Dhabi Grand Prix.
